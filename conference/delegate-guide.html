Overview

The Junior Assembly of the Model United Nations (JAMUN) is a student-led Model United Nations conference designed for middle school students. JAMUN offers young delegates the opportunity to study international issues, enhance their public speaking and critical thinking skills, and engage in respectful, competitive debates with their peers.

Over two days, students represent countries in an immersive simulation of the United Nations, where they debate both historical and contemporary issues. Each committee is staffed and supervised by high school and college students (“chairs”) who spend months preparing to guide and support delegates.

In advance of the conference, delegates will write short essays (“position papers”) to clarify their stance on each topic. These papers help delegates think critically and prepare for negotiation and collaboration during committee sessions. Delegates will draft resolutions, engage in debates, and grow in diplomacy.

Delegates will meet peers from across the country and develop confidence, empathy, and leadership skills.

Key FAQs

  • Conference Dates: November 1–2, 2025
  • Locations:
    • Chicagoland: Deerfield Village Hall (850 Waukegan Rd, Deerfield, IL)
    • Raleigh-Durham: Location TBD
  • Position Papers: Due October 31 at 11:59 PM. Required for awards (except Ad-Hoc).
  • Committee Topics: GA and Crisis topics available on jamun.org/jamun3. Ad-Hoc topic revealed Nov 1.
  • Practice: Ask advisors for simulations. Contact chairs with questions.
  • Committee Sizes: GA: ~40–50 delegates; Crisis: ~12 delegates.
  • Electronics: Not allowed in committee. Bring printed/hard copies.
  • Dress Code: Formal Western Business Attire. Respect cultural variation.
  • Observers: Family/friends welcome during ceremonies. Some sessions livestreamed.

Sample Committee Timeline (GA)

  • Roll call & set agenda
  • Speakers list, moderated caucuses
  • Unmoderated caucuses, drafting working papers
  • Presenting, merging, and finalizing resolutions
  • Voting
  • Repeat for second topic

Crisis committees will include updates and backroom directives every session, in a more dynamic format.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Presence and participation
  • Respect and attitude
  • Creativity and innovation
  • Knowledge of delegation perspective
  • Professionalism and commitment
  • Growth across sessions
