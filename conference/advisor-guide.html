Overview

The Junior Assembly of the Model United Nations (JAMUN) is a student-led Model United Nations conference designed for middle school students. JAMUN offers young delegates the opportunity to study international issues, enhance their public speaking and critical thinking skills, and engage in respectful, competitive debates with their peers.

Over two days, students represent countries in an immersive simulation of the United Nations, where they debate both historical and contemporary issues. Each committee is staffed and supervised by high school and college students (from now on, “chairs”). Our chairs spend the months before conference training to guide discussion and debate, provide guidance, and make sure that all students have the chance to contribute and grow.

In advance of the conference, student delegates will prepare short essays (from now on, “position papers”) that clarify their position on the topics that they will debate. Writing a position paper gives students the opportunity to think critically about the issues that they will face in committee, as well as to develop their stance with care and deliberation. During the conference, following the position they outlined in their position paper, student delegates will collaborate to draft resolutions and directives, building key skills in research, negotiation, and diplomacy.

Student delegates will have their perspectives and opinions supported and challenged by peers from a variety of schools, backgrounds, and cultures. The largest Model UN conference for middle schoolers in the United States, JAMUN gives students the unique opportunity to meet students with similar interests and passions in a variety of states and cities across the United States.
